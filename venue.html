Places to visit in and around San Francisco

Yosemite National Park

Yosemite National Park is a must-see for nature lovers, attracting couples and families alike. See the famous Yosemite Falls which are made up of three separate falls, and the 3,000-foot granite face of El Capitan. Ansel Adams Gallery displays the famous photographer's amazing photos of the park. Evergreen Lodge is a beautiful place to stay right in the park where you can unwind surrounded by trees and the songs of birds.

Half Moon Bay

Half Moon Bay, 45 minutes from San Francisco, features some of the most stunning coastal scenery on the West Coast. Check into one of the ocean view rooms at The Ritz-Carlton Half Moon Bay and schedule a romantic couples treatment at the hotel spa. Stay in and order room service or explore attractions in the area, including the Santa Cruz Mountain Wineries and the Filoli Estate and Gardens. Browse more family-friendly ideas.

Golden Gate Bridge Views

Check into one of the elegant rooms at Cavallo Point and enjoy scenic views of the Golden Gate Bridge on your family getaway. Resort amenities include shuttle service to San Francisco and Sausalito, access for parents to Healing Arts Center & Spa and outdoor meditation pool, morning yoga class and valet parking. The lodge is offering several great packages for families.

Sacramento

If you don't have a car, go on a one-and-a-half hour-train trip to Sacramento where you can go shopping, sample delicious cuisine at local restaurants and visit the California State Railroad Museum. Take the kids to Gunther's Quality Ice Cream and Vic's Ice Cream. The Westin Sacramento has rooms with scenic river views. The newly renovated Hyatt Regency Sacramento is a AAA Four Diamond located near the California State Capitol.

Santa Cruz

Santa Cruz is a great destination for surfing, old carnival rides and vintage shopping. Relax and enjoy the view from Seabright State Beach. Stay at Chaminade Resort & Spa, a AAA 4-Diamond mission style resort in the Santa Cruz Mountains overlooking the Monterey Bay (chaminade.com). The resort features contemporary Spanish furnishings and is close to golf courses, the beach and downtown Santa Cruz. Visit some of the area wineries such as the Santa Cruz Mountain Vineyard which makes European-style wines or Testarossa Vineyard in downtown Los Gatos. Sample local wine paired with cheeses and other delicious eats.

Travel times to other locations in California

Monterey & Carmel - 1 hour and 50 minutes from San Francisco

Monterey offers iconic California experiences. Experience the top road trip in the United States as you wind along the breathtaking Big Sur coastline on Highway One.

Sausalito - 25 minutes from San Francisco from San Francisco

Sausalito is just a ferry ride across the bay and offers unique tours, attractions such as “Bay Model” and “Bay Area Discovery Museum,” enchanting boutique shops, art studios and galleries and a wide selection of restaurants, outdoor cafes and bistros, many with stunning views back across the bay to the city.

The Mendocino Coast - 2 hours and 45 minutes from San Francisco

Shop, eat, and enjoy the feeling of going back in time to a simpler era as you groove to the vibes of downtown Mendocino’s hippy past and quaint New England style architecture.

Yountville - 1 hour from San Francsico

Yountville's compact layout makes it a wonderful place for wandering on foot or by bicycle. Explore the boutiques, galleries and gastronomy-inspired stores like NapaStyle, housed in a winery complex built in 1874.

Napa and Sonoma -1 hour from San Francsico

The hills are fragant with eucalyptus, the lavender-scented valleys planted with row after row of grapevines. It's little wonder visitors are drawn to California's Napa and Sonoma valleys.
